# Community in a Box

## Vision

Community in a Box connects everyday neighborhood sharing spaces into a living digital commons.

Across our cities, small book boxes and swap shelves already exist. They are simple, local, and built on trust. Our vision is to extend them into an invisible digital layer where people, resources, and opportunities can connect.

## The Problem

These initiatives remain isolated.

A book box might be full, empty, or in need of care — but only visible to those who pass by. Nearby neighbors often don’t know what exists just around the corner.

As a result:
- Resources are underused
- Local initiatives stay invisible
- Opportunities for connection are lost

## The Idea

Each place becomes a gateway.

By adding a simple scanpoint (QR code), a book box opens a lightweight digital space tied to its location.

No app. No account. Just scan and access.

## What You Can Do

After scanning, anyone can:
- See what is available or needed
- Share updates or messages
- Discover nearby book boxes and initiatives
- Connect with the local community

## Principles

- Physical first: rooted in real places  
- Zero friction: instant access  
- Local: always contextual to a neighborhood  
- Open: based on simple, extensible protocols  

## H4SG Prototype

During the hackathon, we build:
- A scannable entry point  
- A minimal place page  
- Basic interactions (view, post, discover)  

The goal is to demonstrate how physical places can become connected community interfaces.

## Why It Matters

Community in a Box makes local generosity visible and connected.

It strengthens neighborhood life by turning simple places like book boxes into shared, living spaces — both physical and digital.
